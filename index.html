Brandeburg Gate

A Photograph by John Waterman

Taken From Google Arts and Culture

Why do we choose this item?

Contextualising our project: As we needed at least an item to represent Berlin, we thought that one of the most important cultural sites (and symbols) in Berlin is the Brandeburg Gate.

What about Metadata and Standards?

Searching in the page's Source Code we found that the page has some metadatas using the "OG" standard. This is interesting because even if the metadata are referred to the whole page, the latter contains just the image (and related inforamtion), so metadata describe the item. Then, Google Cultural Institute uses its own "Standard"(in Google Cultural Platform Help page you can find the "Metadata Fields" and "Prepare a metadata file (Google Sheets)"). It seems to be similar (or at least modelled on) Dublin Core.

    Google Metadata Standard (General CSV)
  • title: (dc:title)
  • creator: (dc:creator)
  • dateCreated: (dc:created)
  • provenance: (dc:provenance)
  • subject (dc:subject) = keywords
Issues
  • In the photograph there is a signal: No metadata about it
  • Some metadata fields considered required by Google Standard are not fulfilled (ex: itemid), or maybe not showed up to the final user.

Le vite degli altri

A movie review by Cinematografo.it

Taken From Cinematrografo - Il portale italiano del cinema

Why do we choose this item?

This item is part of the Berlin Wall section. It was chosen as the story portrayed in the movie rotates around the existence of the wall and it can exemplify the influence that the wall had on the movement through the city.

Issues

The item is rich with metadata, though the content provider did not specify the employed standard. To bridge this gap, we described the metadata using a standard of our choice.

What about Metadata and Standards?

The page's source code has metadata following OG standard. A meaningful one is 'type', as it allows to highlight that the information about the movie is intended, as a whole, as an 'article' talking about it. To fill in the requirement of a standard definition of the set of elements used in the article, we employed Ebucore, an ontology for the description of audiovisual resources. It is modelled reusing existing standards Schema.org, Dublin Core and FOAF . This allowed us to indicate a standard also for the media resource specific metadata, as follows:

    Ebucore Standard Metadata
  • Original title: To provide the original title attributed to the media resource e.g. in its original language.
  • alternativeTitle To provide an alternative title.
  • hasMedium: To provide information on the medium formats in which the resource is available.
  • hasColourSpace: To describe the colour space.
  • duration: To provide information on the duration of a MediaResource.
  • dc: creator: A creator of content such as a director or a producer.
  • hasCastMember: A member of the cast.
  • dc:contributor: An Agent who has participated in any phase of management of a BusinessObject or Resource.
  • creationDate: The date of creation of the Resource or BusinessObject.
  • hasLanguage: To associate a Language to a Resource or BusinessObject.
  • hasGenre: To define a Genre/category associated to the BusinesssObject.
  • Country: To provide the country name and or country code.
  • dc:publisher: An Agent involved in the distribution of content.
  • summary: To provide a summary.

Murs

A Video by TV3 Televisió de Catalunya (TVC)

Taken From EUscreen portal

Why do we choose this item?

“30 Minuts visited Mexico, the United States, Morroco, Berlin, Palestine and Israel to see how the lives of the people living there have been affected–often tragically– by the building of walls to separate people.” The video is so representing the concept of Segregation, taking into account the Belin Wall's facts. This item can be seen as a nice way to know more about the Wall through the concept of Segregation and so, trying to let the user start to think about the Wall approching first the "conceptual" and profound meaning at it had (and still has) in society.

What about Metadata and Standards?

EUScreen metadata are modelled through EBUCORE Standard (Here you can find some information about Ebucore and Europeana). Ebucore standard is ment to "provide the framework for descriptive and technical metadata for use in service orientated architectures and audiovisual ontologies for semantic web and linked data developments" (more info). Ebucore is modelled reusing existing standards schema-org, Dublin Core and FOAF.

    Item metadata compared with EBUCORE documentation:
  • OriginalTitle: To provide the original title attributed to the media resource e.g. in its original language.
  • dc:contributor (provider): An Agent who has participated in any phase of management of a BusinessObject or Resource
  • Creation date/time (production year): The date of creation of the Resource or BusinessObject.
  • dc:language: A Language used in the BusinessObject or Resource
  • Summary: To provide a summary (subProperty of Description)
  • Parent season / series (This item is part of a series/collection ):The Episode of a Series or a Season. (+ SeasonNumber, EpisodeNumber)
  • dc:publisher (publisher/broadcaster): An Agent involved in the distribution of content.
  • Broadcast date: The date when the Resource was first broadcast publicly on television or radio or via streaming.
  • Country: To provide the country name and or country code.
  • OriginalLanguage: The original language in which the BusinessObject or Resource has been created and released. This is provided as free text in an annotation label or as an identifier pointing to a term in a classification scheme
  • DubbedLanguage: Dubbed language
  • Coverage (Geographical Coverage): A property to identify the Events and or Locations, all real or fictional, covered by the BusinessObject
  • dc:contributor: An Agent who has participated in any phase of management of a BusinessObject or Resource.
  • Media type: To provide additional information on the type of media
  • Item: An item e.g. newsItem or sportItem -- All the metadata provided from this point are referred to the item (published duration, colour, sound, aspect ratio )
  • genre: To define a Genre/category associated to the BusinesssObject
  • primarytopic (topic): The main subject of interest. (subclass of description)
  • keyword: To associate a concept, descriptive phrase or Keyword that specifies the topic of the BusinessObject or Annotation.

Analysing EBUCORE we found that it is really specific in classifying techincal audiovisual elements (e.g.: subtitle, but also aspect ratio, maximum bitrate, Max momentary loudness) and it has also a really accurate specification of description (e.g: summary, primary topic).

Peter Fechter dies trying to escape

A video by Rbb

Taken From Rbb's 'The Berlin Wall. A multimedia history'

Why do we choose this item?

This item is in the Peter Fechter section. We chose it because Fechter was shot trying to cross the wall: the tragic ending of his life, makes him a symbol of the segregation caused by the division of the city.

Issues

The item is rich with metadata, though the content provider did not specify the employed standard. To bridge this gap, we described the metadata using a standard of our choice.

What about Metadata and Standards?

In the page's source code the source is classified 'type' as a 'website'; this was functional for us to distinguish the metadata strictly refererring to the video, described using Ebucore standard (cf. 'Le vite degli altri'), from those referring to the content of the video (see below).

    Ebucore standard:
  • durationTimecode: The duration expressed as a timecode.

Given that ABitLOD has a focus on the events associated with the Berlin Wall, we decided to assign LODE standard to this item metadata, in order to describe the historical event depicted in the video.

    Ebucore standard:
  • Event: "Something that happened," as might be reported in a news article or explained by a historian.
  • atTime: [The value of this property is] an abstract instant or interval of time that is when an event happened.
  • involved: [The value of this property is] a (physical, social, or mental) object involved in an event.
  • inSpace: The value of this property is] an abstract region of space (e.g. a geospatial point or region) that is where an event happened.
  • illustrate: [The value of this property is] an event illustrated by some thing (typically a media object).

PKW Trabant 601

A small model of the Car

Taken From Museum-Digital

Why do we choose this item?

Mainly for two reasons.  This is a small replica of a specific Trabant car. The fact itself that a replica exists testifies the iconic historical importance of this Car.  The Trabant has been de facto the only car available for private transportation for the people of the DDR. It is also a subject of one of the most famous paintings on the berlin wall.  It was also methodologically challenging to include this item in our project, because its inclusion in our theoretic model requires the formal conceptualization of a replica/imitation of a specific model of a type of car.

What about Metadata and Standards?

We contacted Stefan Rohde-Enslin from the technical team behind the Museum-Digital.de, who informed us about their standard and customizations. Their items are described through LIDO. LIDO is an international Standard controlled by of working group of IDOM-CIDOC. It is based an CIDOC-CRM, an ISO-Standard which is controlled and created by the same working group. The main difference between LIDO and more traditional standards is, that it takes "events" (something that happend with the object) seriously - unlinke dublin core or edm. Taking "events" seriously has the consequence that objects no longer have only one "time" but many. Traditionally (at least in Germany) museum-object-databases have only one field for time.

Scrapbook: Friedrichshain 1970iger Jahre

An album by Haupt Jochen photographer

Taken From FHXB Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg Museum

Why do we choose this item?

We found the item very interesting from different point of views: first of all it is very explanatory of the Cold War time period, exposing more than ninety photographs of East Berlin and its residents in the ‘70s. Moreover, it’s a very challenging item from a metadata point of view: because of its nature as a photo album, it is a physical object that contains writings written on pieces of paper, and also photographs, which make up another item by themselves.

What about Metadata and Standards?

In the blog of the website the research team of the Institution states that they have completed the metadation of all the items, but without saying which was the chosen standard. For this, given the nature of the item, we have decided to use the VRA CORE Metadata standard, which, is a metadata standard for the description of works of visual culture. We found it very interesting because it allows the description of works of art, focusing on work, collection or image.

    VRA CORE
  • Collection = id: 2010/299 source: Collection Heimatmuseum Friedrichshain
  • Worktype = Photo album
  • Title = Scrapbook: Friedrichshain 1970iger Jahre
  • Description = Scrapbook with material from Friedrichshain of the 1970s. Publisher was the council of the district of Friedrichshain, from which the texts were written. The photos are from Jochen Haupt. It contains photos of memorial plaques, Karl-Marx-Allee, Frankfurter Allee, Ostbahnhof, old buildings before the demolition and new buildings around the Ostbahnhof, infrastructure facilities such as indoor swimming pool, child facilities, Feierabendheim, schools and Altbaumodernisierung, art in the urban space, monuments in the Volkspark, from the hospital Friedrichshain and cultural events in the Volkspark
  • Measurements= 30 W 25 H
  • Rights = FHXB Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg Museum
  • Name (subelement of Agent): = Haupt, Jochen
  • earliestDate (subelement of Date = 1961
  • latestDate (subelement of Date) = 1989
Issues
  • The Website doesn’t have an English translation for both the records and the descriptions
  • In the perspective of a future implementation: we could describe both the collection (album), the single images, adding to each one the description of the original author’s notes that are added to the photos of the album - which are now NOT described on the website - using the “Inscritpion” element of VRA
  • We couldn’t find the metadata standard used by the institution for its items

Archivio de L'Unità

A Newspaper's Archive

Why do we choose this item?

We were looking for an newspaper article to put in '9 of November 1989' section, because we wanted to fill in that section using public information coming from that time. The search for items to take from online newspapers' archives was discouraging since most articles, digitazed as pictures of the pages, have no metadata. This question was for us food for thought.

Our Theoretical Model

This model is able to describe the chosen items replying to the questions:
  • WHO – people. How to describe people? Which kind of information?
  • WHERE – places. Which kind of information I have to represent about locations?
  • WHEN – dates. In which format I need to express the notion of time?
  • WHAT – subjects/concepts. What is the main content of my object?
WHO
  • The perspective embraced to describe people in our model is based on thinking that a person is an agent involved in events (birth, professional life, notable involvement, death). Starting from this premise, we chose to use the BBC’s Core Concepts Ontology. On the one hand, we employed properties to describe anybody’s major life events (dateOfBirth, occupation, dateOfDeath). On the other end, we described the implication of the Person in the context of our E/R model (using the property knownFor).
  • Furthermore, we considered to use FOAF in order to link the person to other people and more information and LODE to give more space to the role of a person in an event in order to relate this event to other events, in causal terms. This approach was then abandoned in that choices as such would have provoked an excessively significant intersection with other Ws descriptive categories.

WHERE

At this point we noticed that we need to treat places under many aspects in order to have a broad vision in our model:

  • GEOLOCALIZATION: Places can be seen as points in space. For this reason we want to convey to the user the coordinates of the place of interest. In order to do so we will use wgs84_pos. This standard has an additional useful feature in our model: we can provide a CSV file to indicate a set of points in the space, so to present lines and shapes. This is the specific case of the Berlin Wall building (a set of lines).
  • PLACE AS A CULTURAL SPOT: Our concept model is also based on the idea that the places we present are also places of cultural interest. For this reason we want to provide some useful information about it. In order to do this we will use schema and dbo.
  • POLITICAL LOCALIZATION: To connect this specific place to the city which it belongs we will add some information about the actual city. In order to do this we will use schema providing the specificity of the specification with dbo.
  • HISTORICAL REGION: Considering the fact that we are working on a model which has a strong historical connotation, and considering that we are talking about Berlin in our case-study, we want to add some additional information about the "historical" region, where this place was contained (East Berlin). In order to do so we will use schema joined with owl-time.

WHEN

LODE:
LIDO: Its strength lies in its ability to support the full range of descriptive information about museum objects. It can be used for all kinds of object, e.g. art, cultural, technology and natural science. It is made up of a nested set of ‘wrapper’ and ‘set’ elements which structure records in culturally significant ways. An important part of its design is the concept of events taken from the CIDOC CRM.

WHAT
  • Historical objects can be produced, manifactured, and sometimes even re-produced. Conceptually, their production from a project or symbolic imitations of iconic historical objects can be described by FRBR. "Iconic" objects that are Museal replicas of real objects find expression in FRBR separating the replica from what is its original with the property "imitationOf". The original objects, in turn, are a specific model of realization of some general kind of objects. These levels of realization can be expressively described by FRBR.
  • The LIDO ontology, furthermore, guarantees a powerful expression of event-based descriptions of cultural objects and their history. Object's creation, developments, changes and movements can find expression in this powerful standard that can connect these aspects to places, actors, materials, measurements, general descriptions, processes, subjects and many more. These descriptions can be wrapped in sets and complexes indefinitely, connecting the objects features to relevant historical entities.
